## What Section 4.4 Says

### Search phase

1. **Step 1** ù Minimize \(\sum_{i=0}^{27} H(\Delta W_i)\), with special emphasis on keeping \(H(\Delta W_{16})\) and \(H(\Delta W_{18})\) minimal. These expanded words are largely determined by earlier message words and are harder to control during message modification than \(W_8, W_9, W_{13}\).

2. **Step 2** ù With \(\Delta W\) fixed from Step 1, find \((\Delta A_i, \Delta E_i)\). Three strategies are listed:
   - **Strategy 1:** Minimize \(\sum H(\Delta A_i)\), then with fixed \((\Delta W, \Delta A)\), minimize \(\sum H(\Delta E_i)\).
   - **Strategy 2:** Minimize \(\sum H(\Delta E_i)\) directly.
   - **Strategy 3:** Minimize \(\sum_{i=11}^{27} H(\Delta E_i)\) (later rounds only).

   The paper states **Strategy 3** is best for message modification.

### Correction phase

The Step 2 characteristic is invalid. Correction:

1. **Free** \(\Delta E_8, \Delta E_9, \Delta E_{10}\). **Fix** all other \(\Delta E\), and fix all \(\Delta A\) and \(\Delta W\) from the Step 2 solution.
2. Add **value-transition constraints** for rounds \(10 \le i \le 12\).

The valid final characteristic is **Table 8**.

## `find_dc_model_28_512.py` ù Search Phase

### What the code does

This is **not** the paper's three-step pipeline. It is a **single SAT run** with extensive manual scaffolding.

| Paper | Code | Match? |
|-------|------|--------|
| Step 1: minimize \(H(\Delta W)\) | `ss` hardcodes \(\Delta W_8\)û\(\Delta W_{18}\) from Table 8 | **No** ù W is pre-baked, not minimized |
| Strategy 3: minimize \(\sum_{i=11}^{27} H(\Delta E_i)\) | `obj_value` minimizes \(\sum_{i=8}^{18} H(\Delta A_i)\) via `xd_` | **No** ù minimizes \(\Delta A\), not \(\Delta E\); wrong round range |
| Strategy 1 / 2 | Not implemented as separate phases | **No** |

```python
def obj_value(self, obj):
    # Sums xd_ (Delta A) over steps start_step..end_step-1 (8..18)
    temp += "0bin000000000@%s) = 0bin%s;\n" % ("xd_" + str(i) + "_" + str(j), ...)
```

This is closer to **Strategy 1, phase 1** (minimize \(H(\Delta A)\) with fixed \(\Delta W\)), not Strategy 3.

### Shape / boundary constraints ù partial match

| Paper / attack geometry | Code |
|-------------------------|------|
| Differences vanish in later rounds | `op5`: Method 1 from step **11**; `op2`: Method 1 from step **15** |
| Zero-diff enforcement in SAT window | Steps 4û7 all zero; A zero from step 11; E zero from step 15 (within `end_step=19`) |

### Extra heuristics not in ğ4.4

LOOPs 2û4 in `assign_value()`:

- Flags on `E13 \| E14` with exactly **4** changing bit positions.
- `HW(E12) ? 4`.

These are attack-shape heuristics to steer the solver toward the Mendel et al. collision geometry. They are not part of the three strategies in the paper.

### `ee` / `ss` strings vs Table 8

- **`ss[0]` (W8)** ù matches Table 8 W8.
- **`ss[1]` (W9)** ù does not match character-for-character (Table 8 also encodes fixed **values** as `0`/`1`, which the code's `=`, `u`, `n` alphabet cannot express).
- **`ee[0]`, `ee[1]` (E8, E9)** ù do not match Table 8; rough pre-correction shape.
- **`ee[6]` onward (E14+)** ù align with Table 8 (e.g. E14 matches exactly).

The first two `ee` rows are off; later rounds match. The two files are in sync with each other as a correction workflow, but early E rows are not literal copies of Table 8.

### Likely bug

`obj_value` loops `j in range(32)` on 64-bit SHA-512 words. Even if uncommented, it would only minimize HW on the lower 32 bits of each \(\Delta E\) word (probably copied from SHA-256 scripts).

## Internal Consistency Between the Two Files

The two scripts **are coordinated** as a pair:

| Aspect | `find_dc` | `correct_dc` |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| `ss` (?W) | 11 rows, W8ûW18 | Same 11 rows |
| `ee` (?E) | 10 rows, E8ûE17 | 5 rows, E10ûE14 only |
| Objective | Minimize HW(?A), steps 8û18 | HW(?E) objective disabled |
| Value transitions (`op7`) | All 0 | 1 at steps 10û12 |

Design intent:

1. **`find_dc`** ù rough search with early E heavily pinned; minimize A-differences.
2. **`correct_dc`** ù relax/re-resolve the E8ûE12 region; keep W and late E fixed.

This aligns with "first two rows free in correction, rest constrained" even though the paper says free E8ûE10, not E10ûE11.
